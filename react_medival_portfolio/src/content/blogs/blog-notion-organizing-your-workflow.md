# Notion: Organizing Your Workflow

Notion is the Swiss Army knife of productivity tools. It combines notes, databases, task management, and wikis into one flexible workspace. Let's explore the basics.

## What Is Notion?

Notion is a all-in-one workspace where you can write notes, manage projects, build wikis, and organize your entire life. It's built around blocks — every piece of content (text, image, table, toggle) is a block you can drag, rearrange, and transform.

Think of it as a blank canvas that molds to however you work.

## Pages and Structure

Everything in Notion lives on a **Page**. Pages can contain other pages, creating a nested hierarchy. You can have a "Work" page with sub-pages for each project, each containing their own notes and databases.

The sidebar shows your page tree, so you can navigate quickly.

## Databases

Databases are Notion's superpower. A database is a structured collection of entries, each with properties (text, numbers, dates, checkboxes, etc.).

You can view the same database as:
- A **Table** — classic spreadsheet view
- A **Board** — kanban-style cards (like Trello)
- A **Calendar** — entries by date
- A **Gallery** — visual cards
- A **Timeline** — gantt chart view

One database, many perspectives.

## Templates

Notion has a massive template gallery. Whether you're planning a trip, tracking habits, or building a knowledge base, there's probably a template for it.

You can also create your own templates for repeated use — meeting notes, weekly reviews, project kickoffs.

## Task Management

Use checkboxes, due dates, and kanban boards to manage tasks. The **Board** view with columns like "To Do," "In Progress," and "Done" is a classic setup.

Assign tasks, set priorities, and filter by date or person.

## Study Notes and Knowledge Base

Notion is excellent for organizing study materials. Create a page for each subject, add sub-pages for chapters, embed documents, and link related topics together.

The ability to link pages with `@mentions` creates a personal wiki over time.

> 💡 Tip: Use the slash command `/` to insert any block type without touching your mouse. Type `/toggle` for collapsible sections, `/callout` for highlighted boxes, or `/embed` to pull in external content.
