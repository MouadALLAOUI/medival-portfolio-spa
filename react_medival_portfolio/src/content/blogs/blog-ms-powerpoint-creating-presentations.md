# MS PowerPoint: Creating Presentations

Whether it's a school project or a business pitch, PowerPoint helps you present ideas visually. Here's how to make slides that actually hold attention.

## Starting a Presentation

Open PowerPoint and you'll see options for blank presentations or templates. Templates give you a head start with coordinated colors, fonts, and layouts.

Pick one that fits your topic, then start replacing the placeholder content with your own.

## Slides and Layouts

Each slide has a layout — title slide, title and content, two columns, blank, etc. You can change the layout of any slide by right-clicking it and selecting **Layout**.

Don't use the same layout for every slide. Mix it up to keep things visually interesting.

## Themes and Design

The **Design** tab lets you change the entire look of your presentation with one click. Themes handle colors, fonts, and backgrounds automatically.

You can customize individual elements too — right-click any shape or text box to change its format.

## Transitions

Transitions control how one slide moves to the next. Go to the **Transitions** tab and click different options to preview them.

Use transitions sparingly. A simple Fade or Push looks professional. The 3D rotating cube effect... doesn't.

## Animations

Animations make elements appear, move, or disappear on a slide. Select an object, go to **Animations**, and pick an effect.

Keep animations subtle and purposeful. Animate bullet points to appear one at a time so your audience follows your pacing.

## Presenter Mode

When you present, enable **Presenter View** (automatic on most systems). This gives you:
- Your current slide on the main screen
- Notes and next slide on your laptop screen
- A timer to track your pace

Practice with Presenter View so you're not reading off the main screen like a teleprompter.

## Tips for Better Presentations

- One idea per slide — don't cram
- Use high-quality images (Unsplash, Pexels)
- Minimum font size of 24pt for readability
- The 10-20-30 rule: 10 slides, 20 minutes, 30pt font minimum

> 💡 Tip: Press B during a presentation to black out the screen. It brings attention back to you when you want to make a point without the slides distracting people.
