# Cybersecurity Basics: Protecting Your Data

You don't need to be a hacker to care about cybersecurity. Every time you go online, you're exposed to risks. Here's what you need to know to stay safe.

## What Is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from digital attacks. It's not just an IT problem — it affects everyone who uses the internet.

Good cybersecurity habits are like locking your door. Most of the time nothing happens, but when it matters, you're glad you did it.

## Common Threats

- **Phishing** — fake emails or websites tricking you into giving up passwords
- **Malware** — malicious software that damages or accesses your system
- **Ransomware** — locks your files and demands payment
- **Man-in-the-Middle** — someone intercepting your internet traffic
- **Social Engineering** — manipulating people into revealing information

Most attacks target humans, not computers. Awareness is your best defense.

## HTTPS and Secure Connections

Always look for the padlock icon in your browser's address bar. HTTPS encrypts the connection between your browser and the website, preventing eavesdropping.

If a site doesn't have HTTPS, don't enter any sensitive information — passwords, credit cards, anything.

## VPNs

A Virtual Private Network encrypts all your internet traffic and routes it through a server elsewhere. It's useful on public Wi-Fi (coffee shops, airports) where anyone could be watching.

VPNs aren't magic invisibility cloaks, but they do protect your data from local network snooping.

## Firewalls

A firewall monitors incoming and outgoing network traffic and blocks suspicious connections. Your operating system has a built-in firewall — make sure it's enabled.

Don't turn off your firewall just because an application asks you to. That's almost always a red flag.

## Safe Browsing Habits

- Don't click suspicious links in emails
- Verify website URLs before entering information
- Keep your browser and operating system updated
- Use a reputable antivirus or anti-malware tool
- Be cautious with browser extensions

The internet isn't inherently dangerous, but it does require vigilance.

> 💡 Tip: Use a browser extension like uBlock Origin to block malicious ads and trackers. Many attacks come through malicious ads, not direct targeting.
