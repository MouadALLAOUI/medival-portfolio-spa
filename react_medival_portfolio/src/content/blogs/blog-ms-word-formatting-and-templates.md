# MS Word: Formatting and Templates

Microsoft Word is more than just a blank page. With the right formatting and templates, you can create professional documents in minutes. Let's break it down.

## Text Formatting Basics

The Home tab has everything you need for quick formatting:

- **Bold, Italic, Underline** — the holy trinity of text emphasis
- **Font size and type** — pick readable fonts (Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman)
- **Text color and highlight** — use sparingly for emphasis
- **Align left, center, right, justify** — control how text flows

These are your everyday tools. Get comfortable with them and your documents will instantly look more polished.

## Headings and Structure

Don't just make text bold and call it a heading. Use Word's built-in heading styles (Heading 1, Heading 2, etc.) for two big reasons:

1. They create a document outline you can navigate with the Navigation Pane
2. They let you auto-generate a Table of Contents

Structure your document properly and Word rewards you with features that save hours.

## Styles

Styles are saved formatting combinations. Instead of manually bolding, changing font, and adjusting spacing every time, apply a style with one click.

You can modify existing styles or create your own. Once set up, updating a style automatically updates every instance in your document.

## Page Layout

Go to the **Layout** tab to control:
- Margins (narrow, wide, or custom)
- Page orientation (portrait vs landscape)
- Paper size (Letter, A4, etc.)
- Columns (for newspaper-style layouts)

Setting these up before you start writing saves you from reformatting everything later.

## Headers and Footers

Go to **Insert** → **Header & Footer** to add repeating content at the top or bottom of every page. Page numbers, document titles, dates — all common choices.

Use the **Different First Page** option if your cover page shouldn't have a header.

## Templates

Why start from scratch? Word has hundreds of built-in templates for resumes, letters, reports, and more.

Go to **File** → **New** and browse by category. You can also save your own documents as templates for future use.

> 💡 Tip: Press Ctrl+Shift+8 to show formatting marks. This reveals paragraph breaks, spaces, and tabs so you can see exactly what's happening in your document.
